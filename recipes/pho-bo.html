Ingredientses

  • 1 pound beef bones
  • 1 pound beef brisket
  • 1 pound beef sirloin, thinly sliced
  • 1 large onion, halved
  • 4-inch piece of ginger, sliced
  • 4 star anise pods
  • 4 whole cloves
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 1 tablespoon coriander seeds
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon rock sugar
  • 1 pound rice noodles
  • 1 bunch cilantro, chopped
  • 1 bunch Thai basil leaves
  • 2 cups bean sprouts
  • 4 green onions, chopped
  • 2 limes, cut into wedges
  • Hoisin sauce, for serving
  • Sriracha sauce, for serving

Cooking stepses

  1. In a large pot, bring 4 quarts of water to a boil. Add the beef bones and brisket, and simmer for 30 minutes, skimming off any foam that rises to the surface.
  2. Add the halved onion, sliced ginger, star anise pods, cloves, cinnamon stick, and coriander seeds to the pot. Continue to simmer for another 1.5 to 2 hours, until the broth is rich and flavorful.
  3. Remove the beef brisket from the pot and slice it thinly. Set aside.
  4. Strain the broth through a fine-mesh sieve into another pot, discarding the solids.
  5. Season the broth with fish sauce and rock sugar, adjusting the seasoning to taste.
  6. Cook the rice noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and rinse with cold water.
  7. To serve, divide the cooked rice noodles among serving bowls. Top with the sliced beef brisket, thinly sliced beef sirloin, and chopped cilantro.
  8. Ladle the hot broth over the noodles and beef. Serve immediately, accompanied by Thai basil leaves, bean sprouts, chopped green onions, lime wedges, hoisin sauce, and sriracha sauce on the side for garnish and additional flavor.
